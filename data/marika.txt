Queen Marika the Eternal is the central character in the world of Elden Ring. She is the divine ruler of the Lands Between and the current vessel of the Elden Ring, a god in truth. She grew the towering Erdtree, concealed the Realm of Shadow, and established the Golden Order. By the time of the game's events, she has long vanished from public view, though her influence persists through the Grace of Gold. Her Demigod offspring became shardbearers when they claimed Great Runes that dispersed when she shattered the Elden Ring.

# Characteristics
Queen Marika was a tall Numen woman with fair skin and long, flowing golden hair gathered into two uneven braids, the right being longer. She typically wore a circlet, gold armbands, gold serpentine bracelets, and either a black dress or a robe patterned with golden floral motifs. Often depicted in cruciform poses with her head bowed, she simultaneously evoked divinity, fragility, regality, and martyrdom.

In the present era, her body appears as a stone-like, petrified husk, fractured and cracked like broken porcelain. She is shackled and suspended on a great Rune Arc with a red needle-like shard piercing her abdomen like a spear. During the final confrontation, her body transforms into Radagon of the Golden Order before the Elden Beast reveals itself. Following the player's victory, she ultimately reverts to a fractured, statuesque form.

# Marika's Rune
Marika's Rune was the official seal of Queen Marika, used by her to control vassals, bestow grace, and seal away threats to her power.
Bearing a close resemblance to the Rune of Death, Marika's Rune is a Y-shaped Elden Rune with an upward crescent resembling the lower arc of the Elden Ring, believed to be the basin in which its blessing pool. Its shape parallels Marika's crucified form within the Erdtree. Variations of the Rune recur throughout the Lands Between, such as the Effigies of the Martyr, statues found in churches of Marika, and the numerous crosses found throughout Limgrave and Dominula, Windmill Village.

# Background
Marika came from the Shaman Village in the Scaduview. Her people had an affinity for trees and flesh that melded harmoniously with that of others. The hornsent tortured and sliced up shamans before stuffing them into the jars to be reborn as saints. The Two Fingers recognized Marika as an Empyrean and bestowed her a shadowbound beast in the form of her half-brother, Maliketh faithful follower, incapable of treachery. At some point during this period, Marika took the warrior Hoarah Loux as her consort, who later vowed to become Godfrey, her first Elden Lord Through seduction and betrayal, she ascended to godhood at the Gate of Divinity atop the tower of Enir-Ilim, and became the vessel for the Elden Ring. This affair led to the birth of the Erdtree and Scadutree, and was considered a betrayal by the Hornsent. The Scadutree veiled the land of shadow upon its creation.

# Age of the Erdtree
In the early days, everything was in opposition to the Erdtree. With Godfrey’s aid, Marika waged a War Against the Giants atop the frigid northern mountains, where dwelled the Flame of Ruin capable of burning the Erdtree. Ultimately victorious, Marika was believed to have slain the Fell God of the Fire Giants. Upon learning that the Flame of Ruin would never die, however, she cursed the last of the giants to tend to it for eternity. Marika declared the path to the mountains as Forbidden Lands and excised details of the war from history.

# Liurnian Wars
Queen Marika desired to conquer Liurnia of the Lakes, the land of the Academy of Raya Lucaria and the Carian Royal Family. She declared war on the glintstone sorcerers and sent a golden host led by her secret other half: a red-haired champion named Radagon. The champions of the moon, led by Rennala, repelled the glorious warriors of the Erdtree for a time, but it ended in a draw. In the second Liurnian War, Radagon invades again, but this time, he uses the Celestial Dew at the Church of Vows in a ritual to recant his aggressions and seek absolution. The Carian Queen accepted, and their wrongdoings were washed away. The two were wed and produced three children: Radahn, Rykard, and Ranni.

# Crusade
Marika sent her son Messmer away on a crusade into the lands of the Hornsent. Despite her precautions, her fear of the Abyssal Serpent sealed within him led her to trap him within the Realm of Shadow, where he would wage war to exterminate the Hornsent and devastate their lands. No matter how many of her enemies burned in his flame, however, Messmer received no sign from Marika to end his crusade. Marika kept these events and the land of shadow hidden away and removed from history.

# King Consort Radagon
After Godfrey's banishment from the Lands Between, Marika took Radagon as her second husband and King Consort, elevating him to become the second Elden Lord. Radagon already had three children with Queen Rennala of the Carian Royal Family, and after his union with Marika, she would raise her new step-children, Radahn, Rykard, and Ranni to demigod status.
Marika and Radagon would go on to have two more children of their own, the twins Malenia and Miquella, who like Marika herself and Radagon's daughter Ranni, were Empyreans, and were thus potential successors to Marika. However, Radagon and Marika were in truth the same. Radagon was described as Marika's male "other self". Thus, the twins were the children of a single god, and they were both born afflicted: Malenia with rotting sickness and Miquella with eternal childhood.

# The Shattering
Some time after the Night of Black Knives, Queen Marika used her Numen hammer to shatter the Elden Ring. Fragments of the ring splintered into Great Runes and spread across the Lands Between, starting a conflict between her demigod children. War after war was fought, but there was no victor. For her part, Marika would be imprisoned inside the Erdtree, possibly by the Elden Beast or the Greater Will itself.
