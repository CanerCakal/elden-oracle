# Ranni the Witch
Ranni the Witch, also known as Lunar Princess Ranni and Renna the Witch, is a character in Elden Ring. She is one of the Demigods, and the focus of one of the game's most elaborate questlines.

# Background
A child born of the union between Rennala, Queen of the Full Moon and red-haired Radagon during the age of the Erdtree, Ranni embodied an alliance between Raya Lucaria and Leyndell. She had two older[verification needed] brothers, Radahn and Rykard. As a daughter of Carian royalty, she was a Lunar Princess, and raised in accordance with tradition. Led by the hand of her mother, young Ranni encountered her Dark Moon—cold, dark, and veiled in occult mystery.

The Two Fingers named Ranni as an Empyrean, one with the potential to one day succeed Queen Marika as the divine ruler of the Lands Between, and granted her a Shadowbound Beast named Blaidd to be her vassal. He was approved of by Rennala and was raised as her foster brother. Ranni grew up playing with Blaidd, and they were often accompanied by Iji, a troll blacksmith who served the Carian royals.
At some point, Ranni's father abandoned her mother to marry Queen Marika and assume the title of second Elden Lord. Although Ranni and her siblings were raised to Demigod status by their new stepmother, their heartbroken mother was left indisposed, cradling her Amber Egg in all-consuming grief.

# The Night of Black Knives
Although the Two Fingers had chosen her as Marika's successor, Ranni did not wish to be a puppet for the Greater Will and resented her Empyrean fate. In order to escape it, Ranni stole a fragment of the Rune of Death from Maliketh, the Black Blade and, through a fearsome rite, imbued it into the blades of Numen assassins, who were said to be close to Queen Marika herself. She also gave a fragment to her brother Rykard as a last resort, so he could kill Maliketh if necessary. Ranni plotted for the assassins to kill another individual while she simultaneously killed herself, slaying her own body while the other person died in spirit, thus freeing herself from being an Empyrean.
