The Erdtree is a giant, gold-colored tree that towers above the Lands Between in Elden Ring.

# Overview
The Erdtree is the heart of the Golden Order, and a divine blessing and direct product of the Elden Ring.
The Altus Plateau was regarded as the realm of the Erdtree, and its people believed that those blessed to be born at its foot held a special significance. The blessing granted by the Erdtree and consequently the Elden Ring, is represented by a golden light in the eyes of the inhabitants of the Lands Between, known as the Grace of Gold. Upon their deaths, the remembrances of demigods and greater champions, and other such powerful beings are hewn into the Erdtree.
The entrance to the Erdtree is currently sealed by a latticework of Impenetrable Thorns in the shape of Radagon's Rune.

# Background
The Crucible of life was the primordial matter that became the Erdtree and the place where all life was once blended together. It was said to be close in nature to life itself, and was represented by gold with a reddish tint—primordial gold. Queen Marika transformed the Crucible into the Erdtree under the guidance of the Two Fingers.

# Age of the Erdtree
The birth of the Erdtree coincided with the War Against the Giants. Following the defeat of the Fire Giants and their Fell God, and the confinement of the Flame of Ruin, Queen Marika declared the beginning of a new epoch, glistening with life, known as the Age of the Erdtree.
Although the Erdtree came from a blending of all life, many cultures were deemed vulgar under Marika's regime. In the time of Lord Godfrey, everything once stood in opposition to the Erdtree. Through countless victories in war, it became the embodiment of Order, and defeated were eradicated, enslaved, or banished to the fringes.

# Age of Plenty
When the new age began, the Erdtree flourished with abundance. It secreted primordial life energy in the form of sacred sap, which hardened into an amber that was treasured as the most precious of jewels. These blessings fell like golden tears from its boughs, pooling in flasks or large collection basins. Queen Marika herself personally bestowed them to her Elden Lord and chosen few. These Sacred Tears were worshipped and used to spread the faith in churches across all lands.
During these early days, the Erdtree radiated the gentle warmth of the sun and healed all who basked in its golden glow. Its bark at this time was brown, much like the Minor Erdtrees.
